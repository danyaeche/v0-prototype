The DFM Workflow

The DFM Issue is the
atomic unit of work.

Chorus replaces the CAD → PowerPoint → email loop with a GitHub-Issues workflow for manufacturability. Every manufacturing risk becomes a tracked, stateful issue — raised on a revision, decided by the brand, implemented in a new revision, validated by the partner, and closed. A part is done when its open-issue count hits zero.

CAD→Issues→Resolution→Validation→Closure

1 The atomic unit

Not the document. Not the RFQ. Not the quote. The unit of work is a single DFM Issue — one manufacturability concern, raised against one revision of one part, carried through a defined lifecycle until it is validated and closed.

3 Issue lifecycle · state machine

Open → Accepted → Implemented → Validation requested → Validated → Closed
Reject pathOpen→Rejected— terminal · brand declines the change
Clarify loopOpen→Needs clarification→Open— partner answers, issue re-opens
Failed validationValidation requested→Validation failed→Open— fix didn't hold, back to the brand

Zero open issues → part is manufacturable and released.

5 Two actors, one source of truth

AL
Brand
ALSO · owns design intent
  • Creates projects, parts, and uploads revisions
  • Makes the decision on every issue — accept, reject, clarify
  • Implements accepted fixes in a new revision
  • Cannot validate its own fixes
HP
Manufacturing Partner
Hsinchu Precision · owns manufacturability
  • Reviews revisions and raises DFM issues with recommendations
  • Validates fixes by comparing revisions
  • Confirms the part is manufacturable
  • Cannot see unrelated projects or parts
